Definitions / rules

Clark & Pfeiffer Fish Hatcheries Mentor/Youth Hunts
- Dates (2026): Jan. 3–4, 10–11, 17–18, 24–25, and 31.
- Eligibility:
  - Only youths under 16 and mobility‑impaired hunters may apply.
  - Youths must be accompanied by a mentor (adult 18+).
  - Max 4 hunters per blind, with no more than 2 adults.
  - One mentor may have 2 youth; one youth may not have more than 1 mentor in a blind.
- Registration:
  - Advance online registration only; no standby hunting.
  - Drawn hunters must carry postcard notification during hunt.
- Hunt conduct:
  - Must use assigned established blinds from random pre-season draw.
  - Cannot enter blinds before 5:30 a.m.
  - May only discharge firearms from within blind; cannot change blinds.
  - Max 15 shotshells per hunter.
  - Bag limits same as statewide.
  - Must immediately retrieve downed birds; no chasing/harassing waterfowl outside blinds.
  - Firearms must be encased when traveling to/from blinds.
  - Hunting ends at noon; must be off area by 1 p.m.
  - All decoys/equipment must be removed daily.
- Reporting:
  - Must report harvest immediately after hunt at designated location by submitting permit with harvest recorded.
- Penalties:
  - Violating state/federal regs during a KDFWR waterfowl quota hunt → barred from all other waterfowl quota hunts that year and the following year.

Mobility‑Impaired Hunters
- Eligible for Minor Clark Mentor Hatchery Hunt.
- Must submit copy of mobility‑impaired access permit from KY Fish & Wildlife with application.
- Must follow same hunt rules.
- May only have one assistant.

Hunt Application Procedures (Hatchery Hunts)
- Apply online at fw.ky.gov between Nov. 1–15.
- A youth may apply for more than one date.
- A selected mentor + youth may invite:
  - Either 2 additional youth, OR
  - 1 additional youth and 1 additional mentor.
  - Guest names not required on original application.
- By end of November:
  - Selected individuals receive: quota hunt permit (with date), map, and instructions.
  - Unsuccessful applicants are not notified.
- Preference:
  - Drawn for one quota hunt → lower preference for a second hunt in same season.
- Duplicate applications for same hunt day → disqualification.

Doug Travis WMA Mobility‑Impaired Quota Hunts
- Location: Carlisle and Hickman counties.
- Season: Hunts offered November through January.
- Application: Apply in September.
- Info: Call 800‑858‑1549.

Closed Areas / Waterfowl Refuges
- All designated goose refuge lakes in Western KY coalfields region: closed to all waterfowl hunting.
- Waterfowl refuges (time/area closures):
  - Lake Cumberland WMA:
    - Wesley Bend (bounded by Fishing Creek, Fishing Creek Rd, Beech Grove Rd): closed to public Oct. 15 – Mar. 15.
    - Yellowhole (bounded by Fishing Creek Rd, Hickory Nut Rd): closed to public Oct. 15 – Mar. 15.
  - Ohio River Waterfowl Refuge:
    - From Smithland Lock and Dam upstream to powerline at river mile 911.5:
      - Closed to hunting Oct. 15 – Mar. 15.
    - Stewart Island: closed to public Oct. 15 – Mar. 15.
  - Peabody WMA:
    - Portions of Sinclair and Ken Units posted by signs: closed to public Oct. 15 – Mar. 15.
  - Sloughs WMA:
    - Sauerheber Unit: closed to public Nov. 1 – Mar. 15, except Crenshaw and Duncan tracts (open for waterfowl hunting only).
  - Yellowbank WMA:
    - Signed area closed to public Oct. 15 – Mar. 15.

- Bands must now be reported online at:
  - www.reportband.gov
- Old toll‑free phone number on some bands is no longer for reporting:
  - Callers will be directed to reportband.gov instead.

Pitfalls / common confusions
- Thinking standby hunting is allowed at Clark/Pfeiffer: it is not; must be pre‑registered and drawn.
- Assuming multiple mentors per youth are allowed: rule is one youth cannot have more than one mentor in a blind.
- Forgetting shot limit: each hunter limited to 15 shotshells.
- Misunderstanding closure types:
  - Some areas closed only to hunting; others closed to all public access.
  - Dates differ by area (Oct. 15 vs. Nov. 1 start).
- Expecting notification if not drawn: unsuccessful applicants are not notified.
- Using the phone number on old leg bands for reporting: must use the website instead.
