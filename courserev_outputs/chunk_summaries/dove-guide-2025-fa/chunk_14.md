# Chunk 14 (units 10661-11560)

Key concepts / sections
- Area-specific regulations for:
  - Ohio River (Smithland Lock area; Ballard County)
  - Peabody WMA
  - Pioneer Weapons WMA
  - Robinson Forest WMA
  - Sloughs WMA (Sauerheber, Jenny Hole, Highland Creek, Grassy Pond/Powell’s Lake; plus Crenshaw & Duncan II units)

Definitions / main rules

1. Ohio River closures
- Smithland Lock and Dam upstream to powerline at river mile 911.5:
  - Closed to hunting.
- Stewart (Birdsville) Island:
  - Closed to public access Oct. 15 – March 15, except October muzzleloader season.
  - Shooting hours: ½ hour before sunrise–2 p.m.
  - No entry before 4 a.m.
- Ballard County, in vicinity of Ballard WMA:
  - 50 yards upstream of north border to 50 yards downstream of south border:
  - Closed to all waterfowl hunting Oct. 15 – March 15.

2. Peabody WMA (Ohio, Hopkins & Muhlenberg counties)
- Shooting hours on open portions: ½ hour before sunrise–2 p.m.
- No entry before 4 a.m.
- All users (any purpose) must obtain Peabody WMA user permit ($15).
- Sinclair Unit:
  - Bounded by KY 176, S1 road, Goose Lake Rd. (incl. Goose Lake) and posted signs.
  - Closed Oct. 15 – March 15.
- Ken Unit:
  - Bounded by Wysox Rd., H2, H1, H6 roads (incl. Island and South lakes) and posted signs.
  - Closed Oct. 15 – March 15.
- Maps: available at fw.ky.gov (“WMA Maps”).

3. Pioneer Weapons WMA (Bath & Menifee counties)
- Along Cave Run Lake shoreline bordering area:
  - Breech or muzzle-loading shotguns allowed for duck hunting.
- Elsewhere on the area:
  - Only muzzle-loading shotguns or archery equipment.

4. Robinson Forest WMA (Breathitt, Perry & Knott counties)
- Main block: Closed to all hunting.

5. Sloughs WMA (Henderson & Union counties)
- Units for waterfowl:
  - Sauerheber, Jenny Hole, Highland Creek, Grassy Pond/Powell’s Lake.
- Habitat: seasonally flooded grain, moist-soil veg., marsh, bottomland forest, natural sloughs with cypress/buttonbush; waders recommended.
- Access:
  - Jenny Hole Unit usually requires boat/canoe.
  - Other units may require a boat when Ohio River >30 ft at Evansville or J.T. Meyer gauges (road access may be lost).
- Mobility impaired:
  - One wheelchair-accessible blind on Sauerheber Unit.
- General hours:
  - Shooting: ½ hour before sunrise–2 p.m. CST.
  - Hunters must exit by 2 p.m. daily.
  - No entry before 4 a.m. CST.

Sauerheber, Crenshaw, Duncan II units (Sloughs WMA)
- During duck season (Nov. 27–30 and Dec. 7, 2025 – Jan. 31, 2026):
  - Crenshaw & Duncan tracts: waterfowl hunting only via online quota hunt.
  - Hunting only within designated hunt unit boundaries or blinds.
  - Remainder of Sauerheber Unit: closed to all public access Nov. 1 – March 15.
- Quota hunt application:
  - Period: Sept. 1–30.
  - Results: mid-October.
  - Successful applicants get assigned:
    - Either a hunt unit (may hunt anywhere within its boundary), or
    - A blind-only unit (2 of 12 units: must hunt from blind; no hunting outside).
- Check-in / survey requirements:
  - Selected hunters must check-in Sunday prior to hunt by email to: sloughsquotahunt@ky.gov
    - Include: name, hunt unit, hunt dates, hunt confirmation number.
    - Failure to email check-in = no valid permit (and may be cited).
  - At hunt start:
    - All parties must complete a pre-hunt survey card and submit at designated drop point to check in.
  - After hunting each day:
    - Must complete Daily Post-Hunt Survey by 3 p.m. CST.
    - Failure to complete surveys = ineligible to hunt Sloughs WMA for rest of current and following waterfowl season.
  - Survey cards & drop box: next to kiosk in front of Sloughs WMA office on KY 268.
- Quota permits:
  - Not transferable.
  - No unit/blind may be hunted unless the selected hunter is present (implied by text cutoff).

Examples (implicit)
- If you draw a hunt unit in Sauerheber:
  - You may set up anywhere within that unit’s posted boundary.
- If you draw one of the two blind-only units:
  - You must hunt from the blind; you cannot move outside it to hunt.

Pitfalls / common confusions
- Time restrictions:
  - Many areas have 2 p.m. stop-shooting AND must-exit-by-2 p.m.; plus no entry before 4 a.m.
- Seasonal closures:
  - Stewart Island, Ballard Ohio River stretch, Peabody Sinclair & Ken Units, Sauerheber remainder all have Oct.–March or Nov.–March closure windows—note exact dates.
- Gear restrictions:
  - Pioneer Weapons WMA: breech-loaders only on Cave Run shoreline; otherwise muzzleloader/archery only.
- Permit requirements:
  - Peabody WMA user permit ($15) required for any entry, not just hunting.
  - Sloughs quota permits non-transferable; missing email check-in or surveys can cancel eligibility.
- River level effects:
  - At Sloughs WMA, high Ohio River levels (>30 ft) can cut off road access; may unexpectedly require boat access.
