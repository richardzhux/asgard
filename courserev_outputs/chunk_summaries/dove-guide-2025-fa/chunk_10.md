### Ballard WMA – Waterfowl Hunting Rules

- **Hunt methods**:  
  - Ground blinds  
  - Freelance hunting within designated units  
  - Boat‑in hunting in designated areas only
- **Habitat/conditions**:  
  - Harvested grain fields, flooded crops, moist soil units, natural depressions (“sloughs”)  
  - Woody vegetation, uneven/unsure footing, fast elevation changes  
  - **Chest waders recommended** on all units
- **Accessibility**: 2 wheelchair‑accessible blinds for mobility‑impaired hunters.
- **Daily hunt permit**:  
  - Must draw a daily hunt permit via **online quota hunt system**.  
  - Each drawn hunter may bring **up to 3 guests**.  
  - **$15 Ballard WMA 1‑Day Waterfowl Permit** must be purchased online (fw.ky.gov) prior to hunt; no payment at check station.
- **Shooting time and exit time**:  
  - Legal shooting: **½ hour before sunrise to 2 p.m.**  
  - All hunters must be **off the area by 2 p.m. daily**.
- **Party rules**:  
  - Drawn hunter must be present for party to hunt.  
  - Hunters must stay within **25 feet** of another party member; furthest members no more than **75 feet apart**.  
  - At least **one person ≥ 18 years old** per party.
- **Shell and bag limits**:  
  - Max **25 shells per person per day**.  
  - Bag and possession limits = **statewide limits**.
- **Closed river section**:  
  - Ohio River from **50 yards upstream** of Ballard’s north border to **50 yards downstream** of Ballard’s south border is **closed to waterfowl hunting Oct. 15 – Mar. 15**.
- **Seasonal openings/closures**:  
  - Open for **early wood duck & teal** seasons **with no daily drawing**.  
  - **Closed** for:  
    - **Thanksgiving segment** of the season  
    - **Snow Goose Conservation Order** season

**Common pitfalls:**
- Forgetting to buy the $15 permit online (cannot pay at check station).
- Exceeding 25‑shell limit or party spacing limits.
- Hunting in the closed Ohio River section during Oct. 15–Mar. 15.
- Assuming normal operations during Thanksgiving segment or Snow Goose Conservation Order (area is closed).

### Checking In at Ballard WMA

- **Location**: Check station at **864 Wildlife Lodge Road, LaCenter, KY**.
- **Check‑in time**:  
  - Must check in by **5:00 a.m. Central** (doors open 4:30 a.m.).  
  - Anyone not checked in by 5 a.m. **forfeits** right to hunt that day.
- **Required items**:  
  - All hunters in party: necessary licenses/permits.  
  - Drawn hunter: **driver’s license**.
- **Unit drawing**:  
  - **Random drawing at 5 a.m.** for hunting units.  
  - Hunters pick units in drawing order from available units.  
  - **Unclaimed units** go into a **stand‑by drawing**.
- **Standby hunters**:  
  - Must check in by **5 a.m.**  
  - **Cannot apply for standby at both Ballard and Boatwright on same day**—only one.

**Common pitfalls:**
- Arriving after 5:00 a.m. (lose the hunt).
- Trying to enter standby at both Ballard and Boatwright for the same date.

### Ballard Weather Closures

- **Closures** when:  
  - Floodwaters prevent safe entry by wheeled vehicles, or  
  - Prolonged drought makes areas unavailable.
- **Notification**: At least **24 hours in advance** on fw.ky.gov.
- **Best practice**: **Check website before heading out**.

### Barren River Lake WMA (Allen & Barren Counties)

- **Allowed guns** in specified area:  
  - Breech‑loading or muzzle‑loading shotguns for waterfowl along shoreline bordering the primitive weapons area (“Peninsula Unit”).
- **Sandhill crane restriction**:  
  - No crane hunting **within 100 yards of normal summer pool 552 ft** in **Beaver Creek, Skaggs Creek, Peters Creek embayments**.

**Potential confusion:** restriction is **species‑specific (sandhill cranes)** and **location‑specific (embayments, pool level)**—other waterfowl rules differ.

### Waterfowl Quota Hunts (General)

- **September quota hunt applications**:  
  - For **Ballard WMA** and **Sloughs WMAs**.  
  - Apply **Sept. 1–30**, **online only** at fw.ky.gov.  
  - Can apply for **specific days or blocks of days**.
- **Boatwright online quota hunts**:  
  - Boatwright WMA offers up to **50 hunting spots daily** (water‑dependent).  
  - Apply online **Wednesday–Sunday for the following week**.
- **Standby drawings**:  
  - For **unclaimed spots** at **Ballard, Boatwright, Sloughs** WMAs.  
  - Check each WMA’s section for exact procedures.

**Pitfall:** assuming walk‑up access; many opportunities require prior **online quota application** or standby drawing.
