# Chunk 08 (units 5741-6640)

Key concepts / sections
- Jury instructions on:
  - Elements of animal neglect under Cal. Penal Code § 597(b)
  - Definition of “gross negligence”
- Appellate review of jury instructions
- Doctrine of invited error

Definitions / rules

1. Animal neglect (§ 597(b)) – as instructed
   - A person is guilty if, in a grossly negligent manner, they:
     - Cause an animal to be deprived of necessary sustenance, drink, or shelter; OR
     - Having care or custody of an animal, subject it to needless suffering; OR
     - Fail to provide proper food, drink, or shelter.
   - “Deprivation of necessary sustenance, drink, or shelter” is unlawful when:
     - The person commits an act or omission inherently dangerous to animal life or safety, or
     - The act/omission would inherently produce danger to the animal’s life.
   - “Subjecting an animal to needless suffering” and “failure to provide proper food, drink, or shelter” are unlawful when:
     - The person commits an act or omission which would inherently produce danger to the animal’s life.

   Elements (as instructed):
   1. Defendant had custody of or responsibility for care of an animal.
   2. Defendant committed a grossly negligent act or omission.
   3. That act or omission created a high risk of death or great bodily injury to an animal.  *(“Great bodily injury” language is the contested phrase.)*
