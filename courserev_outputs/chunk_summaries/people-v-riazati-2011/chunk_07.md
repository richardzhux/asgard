# Chunk 07 (units 4921-5820)

Key concepts / sections
- Issue: Proper jury instruction on “gross negligence” for felony animal neglect under Cal. Penal Code § 597(b).
- Competing standards:
  - “Danger to an animal’s life” (Speegle-style instruction).
  - “High degree of risk of death or great bodily injury” (Rodriguez / CALCRIM 970 style).

Definitions / doctrinal rules
- Gross negligence (general criminal law sense – Rodriguez):
  - “An act is criminally negligent when a man of ordinary prudence would foresee that the act would cause a high degree of risk of death or great bodily harm. The risk of death or great bodily harm must be great.”
- Gross negligence (CALCRIM No. 970, shooting firearm grossly negligently):
